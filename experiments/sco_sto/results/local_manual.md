   - Unlike RNNs or convolutional neural networks that rely on sequential scanning or sliding windows (which require positional encoding), Transformers can process input tokens in parallel without explicit positional information.
   - The self-attention mechanism itself encodes the relative positions through the interactions between queries, keys, and values.

   - The Transformer-based model ("the Transformer," specifically TransBERT at the time) outperformed previous models (including RNNs and CNNs) on a range of NLP benchmarks such as:
     - Translation tasks (WMT 2014 English-to-French/German)
     - Language modeling (GLUE benchmark)
     - Question answering

5. **Parallelizability and Scalability:**
   - Transformers can be trained in parallel due to their fully attentional, feed-forward structure.
   - This dramatically speeds up training compared to sequential RNN models.

6. **Generalizable Architecture:**
   - The Transformer architecture is modular and easily extended—stacking multiple layers of self-attention and position-wise feed-forward networks yields powerful sequence models (the "Stacked Transformers" or "Encoder–Decoder" with additional layers).
   - This led to the creation of large-scale language models (e.g., GPT, BERT).

7. **No Specialization for Short/Long Sequences:**
   - The self-attention mechanism handles both short and very long sequences equally well, without needing to switch architectures depending on input length.
